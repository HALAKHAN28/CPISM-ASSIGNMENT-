BRIEF HISTORY OF PUNJAB

Punjab, Pakistan's most populous province, has a rich history shaped by various civilizations. It was part of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization, with key sites like Harappa and Mohenjo-Daro. Following the decline of this civilization, the region became significant during the Vedic period and later fell under Persian and Mauryan empires. The arrival of Islam in the 7th century led to centuries of Muslim rule, including the Ghaznavids and Mughals, who greatly influenced its culture and architecture. In the 19th century, the Sikh Empire emerged, unifying the region under Maharaja Ranjit Singh until British annexation in 1849. The Partition of India in 1947 divided Punjab between India and Pakistan, resulting in significant migration and upheaval. Today, Punjab is known for its agricultural wealth and vibrant cultural heritage.
